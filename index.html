NYC Transit (Easy Guide) ▶

The ONE rule: Use OMNY.

Just tap your credit card or phone on the OMNY reader at any subway turnstile. No need to buy MetroCard. $2.90 per ride. Free transfers within 2 hours. Tap once per person.

For kid: kids under 44 inches tall ride free. No tap needed.

Apps to install:

  • Google Maps — best for subway directions
  • Citymapper — even better, shows live arrivals
  • Uber / Lyft — for late nights or with luggage

Avoid rush hour: 7-9 AM and 5-7 PM (subways packed).

Pro Tips ▶

1. Bring comfortable walking shoes. NYC days = 15,000-20,000 steps.

2. Layers. NYC in May: 50-70°F, can rain. Niagara: 50-65°F, often windy/wet near falls.

3. Power bank. Phones drain fast with maps + photos.

4. Cash $50. Most places take cards but small vendors and tips.

5. Photo ID for adults. Required at observation decks, helicopter, etc.

6. Niagara: bring a change of clothes. Maid of Mist + Cave of Winds = soaked.

7. Statue of Liberty: arrive 30 min early. Security check like airport.

8. NYC: avoid Times Square restaurants. Tourist traps. Walk 2 blocks away for better food.

9. Subway etiquette: stand right on escalators, walk left. Don't block doors. Be quiet.

10. Tipping: 18-20% at restaurants, $1-2 per drink at bars, $1-2/bag for hotel bell staff.
